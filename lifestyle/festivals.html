Malta's Festival Scene

Thanks to 300 days of sunshine and a deeply rooted culture of public celebration, Malta boasts a packed events calendar. The island serves as a major Mediterranean hub for both enormous international music festivals and localized, deeply traditional cultural celebrations.

For expats, attending these events is one of the best ways to integrate, meet people, and experience the diverse character of the islands. While the summer months are densely packed with outdoor events, major festivals run all year round.

Major Music Festivals

Malta has become a premier destination for electronic, pop, and alternative music festivals, drawing massive crowds from across Europe.

Isle of MTV Malta

When: Usually July

Where: Il-Fosos Square (The Granaries), Floriana

One of the largest free open-air music festivals in Europe. Partnering with MTV, Malta brings in global pop and EDM superstars (past acts include Lady Gaga, Martin Garrix, and David Guetta). The main concert is supported by a week of club nights and pool parties known as Malta Music Week.

Earth Garden

When: Early June

Where: Ta' Qali National Park

Malta's largest alternative music and arts festival. It features multiple stages spanning techno, reggae, ska, and world music. The festival has a strong bohemian vibe, complete with an ethnic market, camping areas, and a heavy focus on sustainability and eco-friendliness.

Glitch Festival

When: August

Where: Gianpula Village, Rabat

The undisputed heavyweight of Malta's underground electronic music scene. Glitch attracts hardcore house and techno fans from all over the world, featuring massive stage productions, boat parties, and secret cave raves.

Malta Jazz Festival

When: July

Where: Ta' Liesse, Valletta (Grand Harbour)

Set against the spectacular, floodlit backdrop of the Grand Harbour, this critically acclaimed three-day event brings world-renowned jazz musicians and vocalists to the island for a highly sophisticated, atmospheric experience.

Cultural & Arts Festivals

Beyond the party scene, Malta boasts a rich calendar of cultural and historical events that take over the streets of its ancient cities.

  • Notte Bianca (October): One of the most highly anticipated nights of the year. For one night in October, the capital city of Valletta remains open until the early hours. State palaces, museums, and galleries open their doors for free, while the streets are filled with live music, theatrical performances, and food stalls.
  • Malta International Arts Festival (June/July): Organized by Festivals Malta, this multi-week event features a diverse program of theater, dance, music, and visual arts, often utilizing historic sites like the Hagar Qim temples or Fort St. Elmo as stunning performance venues.
  • Valletta Film Festival (Summer): Malta's largest cinematic event, screening dozens of international feature films, documentaries, and short films in open-air venues across the capital.
  • Delicata Classic Wine Festival (August): Held at the picturesque Upper Barrakka Gardens overlooking the Grand Harbour. Attendees purchase a commemorative glass and enjoy unlimited tastings of local Maltese vintages, accompanied by live entertainment and local food.

Traditional Highlights: Carnival & Fireworks

Two traditional events dominate the local calendar and absolutely should not be missed by anyone living on the island.

🎭 Il-Karnival ta' Malta (February/March)

Dating back to the rule of the Knights of St. John, Carnival in Malta is a vibrant, five-day explosion of color leading up to Ash Wednesday.

Valletta Carnival: Highly organized and family-friendly, featuring massive, brightly colored, mechanically moving floats, competitive dance troupes, and children in elaborate costumes.

Nadur Carnival (Gozo): The "spontaneous" carnival in Gozo is the polar opposite. It is a massive, chaotic, adult-oriented street party known for grotesque, satirical, and deeply bizarre homemade costumes. It's a surreal experience that draws thousands of young people across the ferry every year.

🎆 Malta International Fireworks Festival (April)

Fireworks are a serious business in Malta. This festival takes place over several days in late April, leading up to Malta's EU accession anniversary. Top pyrotechnic companies from Malta and around the globe design incredible, musically synchronized aerial displays, usually culminating in a spectacular grand finale over the Grand Harbour in Valletta.
